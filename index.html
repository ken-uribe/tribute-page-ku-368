Tablo

(Daniel Armand Lee)

Image from Vice By Romano Santos February 24, 2022, 1:36am

Tablo is a powerful figure in Korean music, known
not only for his work with Epik High but also for
his lyrical depth, storytelling, and resilience.

Timeline of Tablo's Life & Career

1980s
// 1980 Born Daniel Armand Lee on July 22 in Jakarta, Indonesia
// — Spent parts of his childhood in Indonesia, South Korea, Switzerland, and Canada due to his father's business.
1990s
// 1990s Moves to the U.S. and later attends Stanford University.
// 2002 Earns Bachelor's and Master's degrees in English Literature from Stanford in under four years.
2000s
// 2001 Forms Epik High with Mithra Jin and DJ Tukutz.
// 2003 Epik High debuts with Map of the Human Soul.
// 2005 Breakthrough success with Swan Songs; "Fly" becomes a major hit.
// 2007 Release of Remapping the Human Soul, which tops charts and wins major awards.
// 2008 Begins hosting Tablo’s Dreaming Radio.
// 2009 Epik High becomes independent under the label Map the Soul, Inc.
2010s
// 2010 Faces the “TaJinYo” controversy over his Stanford education.
// 2011 Clears his name publicly; signs with YG Entertainment.
// 2011 Releases solo album Fever's End (Parts 1 & 2) — deeply personal and critically acclaimed.
// 2012 Epik High returns with 99 under YG.
// 2014 Releases Shoebox with Epik High, featuring hits like "Happen Ending".
// 2015 Publishes Blonote, a book of introspective quotes and radio thoughts.
// 2017 Produces music for other artists, including Taeyang and Lee Hi.
2020s and Ongoing
// 2019 Releases Sleepless in __________ – an introspective, chill album reflecting modern anxieties.
// 2020 Leaves YG Entertainment; Epik High goes fully independent again.
// 2021 Releases Epik High Is Here (Part 1).
// 2022 Releases Epik High Is Here (Part 2).
// 2023 Drops the album Strawberry, featuring collaborations with Colde and Jackson Wang.
// 2024— Continues international touring and festival appearances (e.g., Coachella).
